Heat Pump Systems

Chemisorption, adsorption, and high-temperature heat pump research

Advanced Heat Pump Systems

Decarbonizing industrial heat — which accounts for over 20% of global energy use — requires moving beyond fossil-fuel-fired boilers. High-efficiency heat pumps capable of reaching process temperatures above 200–300°C offer a credible electrification pathway. This research develops next-generation heat pump technologies spanning chemisorption/adsorption cycles, 300°C-class systems, low-GWP refrigerant characterization, and advanced evaporator design for industrial applications.

Research Areas

Chemisorption / Adsorption Heat Pumps

Thermally driven and electrochemical-compressor-driven chemisorption cycles enable heat upgrading from industrial waste streams without electricity-intensive vapor compression. Key work includes reactor bed design with optimized sorbent packing geometries, heat and mass transfer modeling, and experimental validation achieving COP > 1.3 for waste-heat-driven configurations. (Participant, 2021–2025)

High-Temperature Heat Pumps (300°C Class)

Replacing fossil-fuel boilers in chemical processing, food manufacturing, and district heating requires heat pump systems operating above 250°C. This effort focuses on thermodynamic cycle selection, working fluid screening, and prototype system development targeting 300°C-class delivery temperatures. (Participant, since 2025)

Low GWP Refrigerant Systems

Next-generation eco-friendly refrigerants must balance thermodynamic performance, flammability, and environmental impact. Research includes design and commissioning of a vapor–liquid equilibrium (VLE) measurement apparatus for refrigerant blends, mixture property measurement, and equation-of-state development to support refrigerant screening and system optimization.

Falling-Film Evaporators

Falling-film evaporators offer high heat-transfer coefficients at low temperature differences, making them attractive for industrial heat pump condensers and evaporators. Work covers flow visualization, heat transfer characterization, and design optimization for R-1233zd(E) and R-1234ze(E) in horizontal and vertical tube configurations.

Key Achievements

  • Reactor bed design with optimized sorbent packing for chemisorption heat pumps
  • COP > 1.3 demonstrated for waste-heat-driven adsorption/chemisorption systems
  • VLE measurement apparatus commissioned for next-generation low-GWP refrigerant blends
  • Equation-of-state parameters derived for low-GWP refrigerant mixtures
  • Falling-film evaporator design and flow visualization for R-1233zd(E) and R-1234ze(E)
  • Experimental and numerical analysis of pool boiling enhancement on structured surfaces
